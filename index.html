The Undervalued NBA Skills

A look at the metrics that matter for winning but are overlooked by the market.

How This Analysis Was Done

This project was created by analyzing how various statistics correlate with two key metrics: a player's actual on-court value (Player Value) and their actual salary. I then identified the metrics where there was a large disconnect or "gap" between these two correlations. The infographic highlights the five most significant gaps, revealing which valuable on-court skills are being overlooked and undervalued by the current market.

1

Defensive Rating

The biggest gap: 29%

A player's defensive impact is the most undervalued skill in the NBA. While elite defensive numbers have a strong impact on team success, the market does not reward players accordingly.

Examples: Derrick White, Herb Jones, Kris Dunn

2

Effective Field Goal %

The efficiency gap: 17%

eFG% is the best measure of shooting efficiency. Its strong correlation with a player's value is not reflected in its weak link to salary. This indicates the market underrates players who score efficiently rather than in high volume.

Examples: Payton Pritchard, Sam Hauser, Jarrett Allen

3

Two-Point Percentage

Another efficiency gap: 15%

Being an efficient scorer inside the arc is a key to winning, with a positive correlation to Player Value. However, this skill is largely ignored by the market, as shown by its negative correlation with salary. While 3PT% appears to be properly valued, 2PT% is not.

Examples: Goga Bitadze, Jarrett Allen, Ivica Zubac

4

Steals per 36 Minutes

The defensive turnover gap: 13%

Generating steals and creating turnovers is a valuable defensive skill that positively impacts a player's value. But this skill is not adequately rewarded by the market.

Examples: Kris Dunn, Alex Caruso, Gary Payton II

5

Rebounding

The slight undervaluation: 11%

Rebounding generally does not get you paid, as there is 0 correlation between Rebounds Per 36 Minutes and Salary. But it should, as it does improve Player Value and therefore winning.

Examples: Goga Bitadze, Domantas Sabonis, Kevon Looney
